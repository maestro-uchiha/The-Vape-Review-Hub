Nicotine strengths & salts

“Nic strength” isn’t just a number on a bottle|it interacts with power, airflow, wick speed, and your puff style. This guide keeps it simple: what strengths mean, how nicotine salts differ from freebase, and how to pick a level that feels right for your device. General guidance only; follow your device manual and local regulations.

What does “strength” actually mean? #

Strength tells you how much nicotine is in each milliliter of e‑liquid. Most labels show it as mg/mL (e.g., 6 mg/mL) or as a percent (e.g., 0.6%). The liquid’s base (VG/PG) and your atomizer determine how much of that you actually inhale per puff.

  • Lower power & tighter airflow → less vapor per puff → higher mg often feels appropriate.
  • Higher power & big airflow → more vapor per puff → lower mg is comfortable.

Salts vs. freebase explained #

Two common formulations deliver a different “feel” at the same numeric strength:

Type How it feels Where it fits Notes
Freebase Sharper throat hit at equal mg Great in higher‑power tanks at lower mg Common at 1.5|12 mg/mL
Nicotine salts Smoother at higher mg Excellent in low‑power pods/MTL Used at medium|higher mg; observe local caps

“Salts” are nicotine combined with an acid to temper harshness. They don’t add table salt; they tweak pH so higher strengths feel smoother at pod‑level wattage.

Choose by device & draw #

Your device determines how much vapor (and nicotine) each puff carries. Start here, then fine‑tune by how you feel:

  • Tight MTL pods (≤15 W): freebase ~6|12 mg/mL or salts ~10|20+ mg/mL (stay within local limits).
  • Loose MTL / RDL (15|35 W): freebase ~3|6 mg/mL; salts typically ≤12 mg/mL if used.
  • DL tanks (35|80 W+): freebase ~1.5|6 mg/mL; salts rarely used at high power.

If you move from a pod to a DL tank, expect to lower your mg to keep the feel similar.

mg/mL vs. percent #

Most labels use a simple equivalence: 1% ≈ 10 mg/mL.

  • mg/mL → %: divide by 10 (e.g., 6 mg/mL → 0.6%).
  • % → mg/mL: multiply by 10 (e.g., 1.2% → 12 mg/mL).

Examples: 3 mg/mL ≈ 0.3%; 20 mg/mL ≈ 2.0%.

Satisfaction vs. harshness #

If you feel… What it suggests Try this
Cravings soon after puffs; chain‑vaping Strength likely too low Increase mg slightly or tighten airflow; slower, longer puffs
Headache, nausea, dizziness, hiccups Strength likely too high Lower mg, shorten puffs, take breaks and hydrate
Harsh throat at comfortable mg Power/airflow mismatch or high‑PG sensitivity Lower watts, raise VG ratio, or try salts at similar mg

If symptoms persist or you feel unwell, stop using nicotine and seek medical advice.

Picking a starting point #

  • Light user (few cigarettes/day or occasional nicotine): pods ~6|12 mg/mL salts or freebase; DL tanks ~1.5|3 mg/mL freebase.
  • Moderate (half‑pack range): pods ~12|20 mg/mL salts; DL ~3|6 mg/mL freebase.
  • Heavy (pack or more): pods at higher locally allowed strengths of salts; DL ~6 mg/mL freebase then adjust down if harsh.

These are starting points|your best strength is the lowest one that keeps cravings quiet without unpleasant side effects.

Mixing & step‑down #

  • Blend math: Weighted average by volume: 
    Result mg/mL = (A_mg × A_mL + B_mg × B_mL) ÷ (A_mL + B_mL)
    Example: 30 mL at 12 mg/mL + 30 mL at 6 mg/mL → (12×30 + 6×30) ÷ 60 = 9 mg/mL.
  • Step‑down plan: Reduce by 10|25% every few weeks: 12 → 9 → 6 → 3 → 1.5 mg/mL, only when cravings stay stable.
  • DIY caution: If handling concentrated nicotine, wear gloves/eye protection, label clearly, and store locked, upright, out of children’s/pets’ reach.

Quick reference tables #

Device / style Freebase (mg/mL) Salts (mg/mL) Notes
Pod, tight MTL (≤15 W) 6|12 10|20+ Observe local legal caps; salts feel smoother
Loose MTL / RDL (15|35 W) 3|6 ≤12 Airier draw needs less mg
DL tank (35|80 W+) 1.5|6 Rare Most settle around 2|3 for comfort

FAQ #

Is 3% the same as 30 mg/mL? Yes|using the 1% ≈ 10 mg/mL rule, 3% ≈ 30 mg/mL.

Can I mix salts and freebase? You can, but the feel changes. Many blend small amounts to fine‑tune smoothness. Always label the final strength.

Why does my usual strength feel harsher in a new tank? Bigger coils and more airflow deliver more vapor per puff. Drop your mg or shorten puffs.

Any storage tips? Keep bottles sealed, cool, and out of light. Store all nicotine products securely away from children and pets.
