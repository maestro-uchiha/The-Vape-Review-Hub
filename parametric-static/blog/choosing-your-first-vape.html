Choosing your first vape

Buying your first vape can feel like alphabet soup|pods, MTL, DL, salts, coils. This guide trims the jargon and helps you choose a simple, satisfying setup that fits your style, budget, and goals. Regulations and age limits vary by region; follow your local laws and product instructions.

Start with your goals #

  • Convenience first? You’ll want something small, leak‑resistant, and easy to refill or swap.
  • Strong, cigarette‑like puffs? Look for MTL (mouth‑to‑lung) pods/coils and tighter airflow.
  • Big clouds & bold flavor? You’re in DL (direct‑lung) territory with more airflow and power.
  • Budget control? Refillable pods and replaceable coils beat disposables on cost over time.

Device types at a glance #

Disposable Pre‑filled, single‑use device Zero setup, small Highest cost per mL; waste; limited flavor/nic options Absolute simplicity on the go

DL/sub‑ohm kit High‑airflow tank + more power Dense vapor, saturated flavor Uses more e‑liquid; stronger throat hit at same mg Cloudy flavor chasers

Nicotine & throat‑hit basics #

Strength is labeled as mg/mL or % (1% ≈ 10 mg/mL). Lower‑power MTL devices typically use higher strengths; high‑power DL setups use lower strengths because each puff delivers more vapor.

Device style Common range (freebase) Common range (salts) Notes
Tight MTL pods (≤15 W) 6|12 mg/mL 10|20+ mg/mL* *Observe local limits; salts feel smoother at higher mg
Loose MTL / RDL (15|35 W) 3|6 mg/mL ≤12 mg/mL Airier draw needs less strength
DL tanks (35|80 W+) 1.5|6 mg/mL Rare Most choose 2|3 mg/mL for comfort

If you ever feel dizzy or nauseous, stop and lower your strength. If you crave constantly, consider a small bump up or shorter sessions.

E‑liquid 101 #

  • VG/PG ratio: Higher VG = thicker, smoother, bigger clouds; higher PG = thinner, sharper throat hit, carries flavor. MTL pods often like 50/50; DL tanks like 60|70% VG.
  • Flavor families: Tobacco, menthol, fruits, desserts, beverages. Dark, sweet mixes can gunk coils faster.
  • Freebase vs salts: Salts feel smoother at higher mg for low‑power pods. Freebase is common at lower mg in higher‑power gear.
  • Allergies/sensitivities: If PG feels harsh, try a higher‑VG blend; hydrate and take breaks.

Battery safety & charging #

  • Use quality USB power sources and the cable supplied by the manufacturer.
  • For dual‑battery mods, prefer an external, reputable charger and married pairs of cells.
  • Never carry loose cells with keys/coins|use a plastic case. Replace torn wraps immediately.
  • Don’t charge on soft or flammable surfaces and unplug when full.

Setup primer: first fill & first puffs #

  1. Install the coil/pod. If the coil is separate, press‑fit firmly per the manual.
  2. Prime the wick. Add a few drops of e‑liquid directly to the visible cotton (if accessible).
  3. Fill the tank/pod and cap tightly. Let it sit 5|10 minutes.
  4. Set power low (or use the device’s auto setting). Take a few gentle primer puffs.
  5. Increase slowly until flavor and warmth feel right|avoid chain‑puffing for the first few mL.

Troubleshooting: quick fixes #

Problem Likely cause Fix
Burnt taste Wick not saturated; power too high; coil at end of life Lower watts, wait longer between puffs, replace coil
Leaking Loose seals, thin (high‑PG) liquid, damaged pod Check O‑rings, use slightly higher‑VG, replace pod
Spitback/gurgle Flooded coil, over‑primed, low power Blow through mouthpiece with device off, raise power a touch
Weak flavor Old coil, airflow too open, low power Replace coil, narrow airflow, nudge power up

Quick picks by persona #

  • “I want the simplest start.” Refillable pod kit with built‑in coil pods; 50/50 e‑liquid at a moderate strength appropriate for MTL.
  • “I want strong, cigarette‑like pulls.” Tight‑draw MTL pod or MTL tank on a small mod; higher mg within local limits.
  • “I’m here for flavor.” Air‑adjustable pod with replaceable coils or a compact DL kit; lean toward cleaner, less sweet liquids to protect coil life.
  • “I hate charging daily.” Pod with 900|1500 mAh battery or a single‑battery mod with USB‑C; carry a spare pod/coil.
  • “I’m budget‑focused.” Refillable pod + multi‑pack of coils; buy e‑liquid in larger bottles where legal.

FAQ #

How long does a coil last? Typically 1|3 weeks, depending on power, sweetness of the liquid, and puff frequency.

Can I use any e‑liquid in any device? Check your manual. Thicker, high‑VG liquids may struggle in tight, small‑wick pods; thinner 50/50 blends are more universal.

Is a bigger number better for nicotine? Not necessarily. Match strength to device and comfort. If you feel unwell, stop and lower your strength.

Do I need spare parts? It’s smart to keep a spare pod/coil and a small bottle of e‑liquid. If your device uses removable batteries, a case for safe carry is essential.
