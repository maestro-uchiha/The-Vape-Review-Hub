Nicotine strengths decoded

“What nicotine strength should I use?” is the most common vape question|and the most confusing. This guide decodes labels, explains the difference between mg/mL and percent, and helps you pick a strength that fits your device, puff style, and goals. It’s general guidance only; always follow local regulations and your device’s safety limits.

Nicotine basics #

Nicotine strength on an e‑liquid bottle tells you how much nicotine is present in each milliliter of liquid. Two common ways to display it:

  • mg/mL (milligrams per milliliter), e.g., 6 mg/mL.
  • % (percent by volume/weight approximation), e.g., 0.6%.

They represent the same thing|just different formats. Strength alone doesn’t define “harshness” or satisfaction; power level, airflow, coil type, and puff duration all change how strong a vape feels.

mg/mL vs percent: easy conversions #

Most labels use a simple rule of thumb:

  • 1% ≈ 10 mg/mL
  • mg/mL → %: divide by 10 (e.g., 6 mg/mL → 0.6%).
  • % → mg/mL: multiply by 10 (e.g., 1.2% → 12 mg/mL).

Examples: 3 mg/mL ≈ 0.3%; 20 mg/mL ≈ 2.0%.

Freebase vs nicotine salts #

There are two common formulations:

Type Feel Typical use Notes
Freebase More throat hit at the same mg Lower strengths in higher‑power tanks Clean, punchy; common at 1.5|12 mg/mL
Nicotine salts Smoother at higher mg Pods/MTL at lower power Used at medium|higher mg; follow local limits

“Salts” aren’t table salt|they’re nicotine combined with an acid to adjust pH. That reduces harshness, so higher strengths feel smoother in low‑power devices. At high wattage, those same strengths can feel overwhelming; choose accordingly.

Match strength to device & style #

Your device and airflow determine how much vapor (and nicotine) you inhale per puff. Use this as a starting point, then adjust by feel:

  • Tight MTL pods/coils (≤15 W): freebase ~6|12 mg/mL or salts ~10|20+ mg/mL (stay within local caps). Short, cigarette‑like puffs.
  • Loose MTL / RDL (15|35 W): freebase ~3|6 mg/mL; salts typically on the lower end (≤12 mg/mL) if used at all.
  • DL tanks (35|80 W+): freebase ~1.5|6 mg/mL, most settle at 2|3 mg/mL to avoid harshness with big lung inhales. Salts are rarely used here.

If you’re switching devices, re‑evaluate your strength. The right number for a pod may feel far too strong in a cloud‑chasing tank.

Choose by habit: light, medium, heavy #

Past smoking patterns can help you pick a starting point (then tune by feel and device):

  • Light user (formerly a few cigarettes/day or casual nicotine use): pods ~6|12 mg/mL salts or 6|9 mg/mL freebase; DL tanks ~1.5|3 mg/mL freebase.
  • Moderate user (half‑pack range): pods ~12|20 mg/mL salts; DL tanks ~3|6 mg/mL freebase.
  • Heavy user (pack or more): pods at higher locally allowed strengths of salts; DL tanks ~6 mg/mL freebase as a starting point, then reduce if harsh.

These are starting points, not rules. The goal is steady satisfaction without dizziness or nausea.

Too much vs too little: signs to watch #

Too little Too much
Frequent cravings, chain‑puffing, irritable, reaching for stronger puffs Headache, nausea, dizziness, rapid heartbeat, hiccups, harshness
Solution: raise strength slightly or take longer pauses between puffs to reassess Solution: lower strength, shorten puffs, or use fewer puffs; hydrate and take a break

If symptoms persist or you feel unwell, stop using nicotine and seek medical advice.

Mixing and stepping down safely #

  • Simple blending: Equal parts 12 mg/mL and 6 mg/mL make 9 mg/mL. Weighted averages work: (Amg×AmL + Bmg×BmL) ÷ total mL.
  • Step‑down plan: Reduce by 10|25% every few weeks. Example: 12 → 9 → 6 → 3 → 1.5 mg/mL, adjusting only when cravings are stable.
  • DIY caution: If handling concentrated nicotine, wear gloves/eye protection, label clearly, and store locked, upright, away from children and pets.

Quick reference tables #

Device / style Typical strength (freebase) Typical strength (salts) Notes
Pod, tight MTL (≤15 W) 6|12 mg/mL 10|20+ mg/mL* *Stay within local legal limits; higher mg feels smoother with salts
Loose MTL / RDL (15|35 W) 3|6 mg/mL ≤12 mg/mL (if used) Airier draw reduces needed strength
DL tank (35|80 W+) 1.5|6 mg/mL Rare Most settle at 2|3 mg/mL for comfort

FAQ #

Is 3% the same as 30 mg/mL? Yes|using the 1% ≈ 10 mg/mL rule, 3% ≈ 30 mg/mL.

Why does my usual strength feel harsher in a new tank? Bigger coils and more airflow deliver more vapor per puff. Drop your mg or shorten puffs.

Can I mix salt and freebase? You can, but the feel changes. Many users blend small amounts to fine‑tune smoothness; always label the result with the new total mg/mL.

Any storage tips? Keep bottles sealed, cool, and out of light. Store all nicotine products securely away from children and pets.
